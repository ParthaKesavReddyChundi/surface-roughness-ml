📖 Parameter Guide

Optimal settings, physical effects, and Ra quality reference.

⚡ Print Speed

Typical: 30–100 mm/s

Higher speeds increase vibration and reduce deposition consistency, generally increasing Ra.

💡 Start at 60 mm/s. Reduce to 40 mm/s for fine-detail parts.
30
100

📐 Layer Height

Typical: 0.1–0.4 mm

Most influential parameter. Larger layers = higher Ra. Thinner layers = smoother but slower.

💡 0.1–0.15 mm for smooth finish; 0.2 mm for balanced quality.
0.1
0.4

🌡 Nozzle Temperature

Typical: 190–230 °C (PLA)

Affects filament viscosity. Too low → poor adhesion; too high → stringing. Both increase Ra.

💡 200–210 °C optimal for PLA. Test in 5 °C increments.
190
230

〰 Vibration Frequency

Accelerometer reading (Hz)

Structural vibration causes micro-errors in deposition. Higher vibration → higher Ra. Unique to this project.

💡 Dampen printer with rubber feet. Tighten frame bolts before printing.
30
400

Quality thresholds used in this project.
